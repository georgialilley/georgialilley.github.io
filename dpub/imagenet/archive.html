GRAFFITI AS EXPRESSION.
For as long as people have been using the streets as their canvas, there has been the continued debate about whether graffiti is to be considered vandalism or art? While I haven't formed a strong opinion to this question yet, I find the role that graffiti plays in the life of our cities extremely interesting. There’s a raw and brutal honesty found in the words on passing trains and laneway walls, words that give meaning and asks questions of the faces within the concrete jungle.

In response to my curiosity to understand the motivation behind such vandalism, I’ve curated this digital archive of graffiti phrases that I find inspiring, entertaining and reflective. To me, the artists’ sporadic decisions to sprawl words in public often help create a representation of society at that exact moment in time. Each example plunges me into a spiral of thought about the context of the words, but the anonymity of each artist makes finding answers near impossible and helps to maintain an aura of mystery.

Graffiti not only lets the people speak, but it lets them be heard. Words With Walls is a collection that explores this dialogue we have with the streets and the place it holds in our society.
